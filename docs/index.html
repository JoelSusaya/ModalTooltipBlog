Figuring out Factorio

Do we really need another early-access, base-building sandbox?

We won't after Factorio.

REVIEW
By Joel Susaya | August 1, 2017 10:10PM EDT
Factorio

Factorio

Platform: Windows, Mac OS X, SteamOS + Linux

Developer: Wube Software LTD.

Factorio-Related Pages

Factorio is 2D, top-down base building game similiar to games like Minecraft and Space Engineers. Stranded on a planet, the player has to harness the power of the world's resources to research and build a rocket off the foresaken rock.

Base Building

Base building games generally take place in a sandbox where the player creates an ever-expanding base to tackle larger and larger problems.

More Base Building Games

In traditional base building games, the player often is tasked with the collection of resources and subsequently crafting them. It is generally a very manual task, but one that gamers often find rewarding as we watch our creations increase in complexity.

Through its use of conveyer belts, robotic arms, and other such mechanics, Factorio empowers the player to create fully automated systems to harvest, process, and fabricate items. Need fuel for your machinery to run? Automate the process by feeding a conveyor belt of coal to the machines. You can even automate your factory to create the research you need to unlock more equipment for deeper automatization.

Research

Research is one of the primary goals the player has in Factorio. Each research the player completes helps to unlock further research, which ultimately leads to the victory condition where the player builds a rocket ship.

If you're worried about being out of a job, you can relax as there's always more to do in Factorio. Whether it's optimizing the layout of your conveyor system or enhancing your base's electric infrastructure, you can always find something that needs improvement. Just be mindful of your carbon footprint, or you may discover that your neighbors aren't big fans of machines.
